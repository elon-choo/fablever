On macOS:

```bash
lsof -ti :8080 | xargs kill
```

- `lsof -ti :8080` lists just the PID(s) listening on that port (`-t` = terse/PID-only).
- `xargs kill` sends them `SIGTERM`.

If a process won't die gracefully, force it:

```bash
lsof -ti :8080 | xargs kill -9
```

Quick variants:
- **See what it is first** (recommended before killing): `lsof -i :8080`
- **One-liner with guard** (avoids `xargs` erroring on no match): `kill $(lsof -ti :8080)`
- **Newer macOS / Linux alt:** `fuser -k 8080/tcp`
