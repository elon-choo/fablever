For a content-heavy marketing site, **server-side rendering — and ideally static generation (SSG) — is almost certainly the right call.** CSR is the wrong fit here. Here's the reasoning:

## Why not CSR

A client-rendered app ships a near-empty HTML shell plus a JS bundle, then fetches and renders content in the browser. For a marketing site that's a bad trade:

- **SEO suffers.** Crawlers and link-preview bots (Google, Slack, Twitter/X, LinkedIn) see an empty shell on first load. Google *can* execute JS, but it's slower, flakier, and many social/preview bots don't run JS at all — so your Open Graph cards and meta tags break.
- **Slow first paint.** Users stare at a blank screen until the bundle downloads, parses, and the data fetch resolves. Marketing traffic is often mobile / cold-cache / ad-clicks — exactly the audience that bounces on slow loads.
- **Content is the product.** On a marketing site the content *is* the thing. Making it depend on JS execution adds fragility for no benefit.

## What to actually pick

For mostly-static content (landing pages, blog, docs, pricing):

- **SSG / prerendering** is the sweet spot. Pages are built to HTML ahead of time and served from a CDN — fastest possible load, perfect SEO, trivial to scale, cheap to host.
- **SSR** when content is dynamic/personalized or updated frequently enough that rebuilds are impractical (e.g. per-request data, A/B logic, localization).
- **ISR / on-demand revalidation** (Next.js and similar) blends both: static speed with periodic or triggered regeneration. Great default for a CMS-backed marketing site.

In practice you don't pick one globally — frameworks let you choose per-route. Static-generate the landing/blog pages, SSR the few dynamic ones, and hydrate interactive islands (nav, forms, animations) on top.

## Rule of thumb

| Signal | Lean SSG/SSR |
|---|---|
| SEO / shareability matters | ✅ strongly |
| Content mostly static | ✅ SSG |
| Content dynamic/personalized | ✅ SSR or ISR |
| Highly interactive app behind login (dashboard) | CSR is fine there |

**Bottom line:** go SSR, and prefer static generation (SSG/ISR) wherever the content allows. Reserve CSR for interactive, authenticated app sections — not for the marketing pages themselves.
