On macOS (your setup):

```bash
lsof -i :3000
```

Add `-t` to get just the PID — handy for piping into kill:

```bash
kill -9 $(lsof -t -i:3000)
```

A couple of alternatives:

- **`lsof -nP -i :3000`** — `-n` skips DNS, `-P` skips port-name lookup, so it returns faster and shows the raw number.
- **`netstat -anv | grep 3000`** — no extra install, but noisier and doesn't show the process name directly.

`lsof -i :3000` is the one to remember. The output's `PID` and `COMMAND` columns tell you exactly what's holding the port.
