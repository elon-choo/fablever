The single most important first move: **measure before you change anything.** "Feels slow" has at least three very different causes, and guessing wastes time. Here's the fast triage:

## 1. Open DevTools and find *where* the time goes
- **Network tab** (reload with cache disabled): Is the page waiting on a slow server response (long TTFB), or downloading too much (big JS bundles, unoptimized images)?
- **Lighthouse / Performance tab**: Run it once. It'll tell you if the bottleneck is load time, rendering, or JavaScript execution, and give concrete numbers (LCP, TBT, CLS).

This 5-minute step tells you which of the three buckets you're in:

| Symptom in DevTools | Likely cause | 
|---|---|
| High TTFB (server slow to respond) | Backend / DB query / N+1 |
| Large transfer size, slow downloads | Bundle size, images, no compression |
| Page loads fast but feels janky | Client-side JS, re-renders, blocking main thread |

## 2. Fix the biggest number first
Don't optimize everything — find the one metric that's worst and chase that. Usually it's one of:
- **Slow backend** → look at the slowest endpoint, check for unindexed queries / N+1.
- **Heavy frontend** → code-split, lazy-load, compress images, enable gzip/brotli.
